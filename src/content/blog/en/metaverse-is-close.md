When you hear the word "metaverse," you may strongly picture putting on a VR headset and stepping into a virtual world. Facebook changed its name to Meta and is putting effort into Horizon Worlds, but to be honest, many people probably still don't quite feel it.

## Microsoft Actually Has a 100-Million-Person Metaverse

But if you change your perspective, you realize that "a huge metaverse already exists." That's Minecraft.

Minecraft has more than 100 million monthly active users. Players build, go on adventures, and interact with other players inside a virtual space. In essence, that is the metaverse itself. You can access it from a PC or smartphone even without a VR headset.

Meta's Horizon Worlds has also recently become accessible from a browser without VR. I think the gateway to the metaverse is not a "VR device," but rather a "virtual space where people gather."

![VR headsets aren't the only gateway to the metaverse](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1622979135225-d2ba269cf1ac?w=800&h=400&fit=crop&q=80)

## The Potential of Communities Built with Minecraft

Seen that way, simply running a Minecraft server means you already have one small metaverse. Aceserver, operated by Acecore, is also a virtual space where players interact, build together, and enjoy events.

For information on how to join and an overview of the server, please also see the [Aceserver section on the services page](/services/#aceserver).

The metaverse isn't just something large corporations build by spending hundreds of billions of yen. It already exists as an extension of community. We hope to find even more interesting ways to make use of this familiar kind of metaverse.
