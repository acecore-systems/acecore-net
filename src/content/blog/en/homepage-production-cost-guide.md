One of the most common questions when consulting about website production is "how much will it cost?" The short answer is: cost is not determined by page count alone. It varies significantly depending on the site's purpose, depth of design, use of a CMS, copywriting, photography, SEO, and whether post-launch maintenance is included.

## Estimated Costs by Purpose

The following is a general guide based on common configurations for small and medium-sized businesses, shops, and organizations. Actual prices vary depending on the availability of copy and photos, design requirements, and functional requirements such as contact forms and CMS.

| Type | Main Purpose | Common Configuration | Estimated Cost |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Simple company profile site | Providing company information, digital business card | Top, About, Services, Portfolio, Contact | ¥300,000–¥800,000 |
| Corporate site | Generating inquiries, recruitment, building trust | 5–15 pages, portfolio, blog, CMS | ¥800,000–¥2,000,000 |
| Landing page | Driving sign-ups from ads or campaigns | Single page, CTA, form integration | ¥200,000–¥800,000 |
| Recruitment / service-specific site | Strengthening recruitment applications or resource requests | Dedicated funnel, photography, interviews, forms | May exceed ¥1,000,000 |

While low-cost options exist, a site that cannot be updated after launch, is hard to read on smartphones, or fails to generate inquiries will yield a poor return on investment. Even when keeping initial costs down, it is important to build a structure that allows for future improvements.

## What to Check in a Quote

When reviewing a website production quote, do not just look at the total — verify what is and is not included. The following items are areas where quotes tend to differ significantly.

- **Requirements definition & design**: The process of clarifying objectives, target audience, page structure, and user flows
- **Design**: Whether templates are used or an original design is created
- **Copywriting**: Whether the client provides copy or writing support is included
- **Photos & images**: Whether existing assets, stock photos, or a new photo shoot will be used
- **CMS**: Whether a system for self-managed news or blog updates is included
- **SEO**: Whether titles, structured data, page speed, and internal linking are addressed
- **Forms & integrations**: Whether contact, reservation, resource request, and CRM integrations are included
- **Maintenance & operations**: Whether post-launch updates, backups, security management, and improvement proposals are included

Quotes that only say "all-inclusive" tend to generate additional charges later. Clearly defining the production scope, number of revisions, deliverables, and post-launch support scope makes decision-making easier for both client and vendor.

## How to Achieve Results While Keeping Costs Down

It is not necessary to build every page from the start. Prioritizing pages directly tied to outcomes — such as inquiries or recruitment — and gradually adding case studies and blog posts after launch is also an effective approach.

For example, you can narrow the initial launch to:

1. The top page to communicate the business's value and strengths
2. A service page with dedicated funnels for different inquiry types
3. Portfolio / case study pages to reinforce credibility
4. A contact page with an easy-to-use form
5. A blog to accumulate search traffic and demonstrate expertise

## Prioritize "Operable" Over "Cheap"

A website is not finished at launch. To increase search traffic, you need to continuously review service information, add case studies, publish articles, and improve page speed and form performance.

When comparing production costs, considering the following post-launch operational costs reduces the risk of failure.

- Who will update news and blog posts
- How quickly minor site edits can be handled
- Whether improvement proposals based on access analytics are available
- Who manages domain, server, SSL, and backups
- How spam submissions and security for contact forms are handled

With a sustainable update workflow in place, your website can grow as a business asset. Conversely, when updates stop, information becomes outdated and affects both credibility and search rankings.

## Summary

Website production costs vary by purpose, scope, and operational structure. What matters is not just whether the price falls within a typical range, but whether the design and operational support necessary for your goals are included.
